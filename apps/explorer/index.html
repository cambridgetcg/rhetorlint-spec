Strip — the de-spun text

Deterministic, no model: adverbial spin (intensifiers, deniable adverbs) is subtracted and each agentless passive is flagged with [who?]. Verb-phrase hedges are left marked, not paraphrased — strip subtracts spin, it never rewrites for you.

How to read this tool honestly

  • DOESMark tells in the words — every mark points at a real, visible phrase you can see and check.
  • DOES NOTRead the person. It infers no tone, no intent, no deception. A tell is not a lie.
  • DOES NOTJudge whether a claim is factually true. That is your job, and the world's — not a pattern-matcher's.
  • DOESReport confidence as heuristic — a language-pattern likelihood, never a probability that anyone is lying.

A high density means the language is thickly styled, not that the speaker is dishonest. Honest people use hedges; liars sometimes speak plainly. Captioneer gives you a lens, not a verdict.

engine · rules

Captioneer marks language patterns. It does not detect lies, read intent, or verify facts. Confidence is heuristic. This page makes no accuracy claims — it is a reading aid, not a truth machine. Built to run entirely on your device.
